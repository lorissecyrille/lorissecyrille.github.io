WHAT OUR FANTASTIC USERS SAY

learn more and be inspired more

"I was influenced by my friend during my grade 9 days. I also wanted a sport to play because basketball was not for me. Once I played it frequently, I soon fell in love with it and have never stopped since.
My body became more athletic and my vertical jump became higher. My endurance also increased. And most of all, I gathered a lot of friends through playing volleyball.
My position is middle blocker.
The middle blocker's position is to become the defense along the net. I need to block opposing spikes or make them easier to be dug by the receivers in the back row of the court. I also need to become a decoy (by approaching for a quick attack) to fake opposing blockers and make our wing spiker's job easier by freeing up the defense of the opposing team."

Sean Michael L. Bote
STUDENT/ATHLETE, 12TH GRADE

"I started playing volleyball when I was in my 5th grade. My neighbor bought a volleyball and asked me to play with him, so I did. I started watching volleyball leagues on TV and started to idolize some of the players. I once dreamed to be a volleyball star that will be seen on national TV so I push myself to play volleyball and started to fell in love with it. I continue playing volleyball until now that I'm on my 10th grade.
There are lots of benefits that you will get in playing this kind of sport. My body became broader compared before. My agility and endurance were trained professionally. Joining volleyball competitions has also many benefits like having your school's jersey for free (jacket, jogging pants, shorts, tshirts, etc.). There are also incentives given whenever you win that competiton. The best benefit in playing this sport is having a lot of friends inside and outside the court. Playing volleyball gives so much benefits to me.
My position in volleyball is outside hitter or sometimes called the open spiker.
In volleyball, the open spiker or outside hitter is responsible for attacking and scoring for the team. This spiker is usually seen in zone 4 of the volleyball court (left side near the net). This position blocks the opposite spiker of the other team. Being an open spiker, I need to dig the ball coming from the open spiker of the other team going cross court."

HAROLD DAOS
STUDENT/ATHLETE, 1OTH GRADE
